    Religious festivals are commonly celebrated on recurring cycles in a calendar year or lunar calendar.

    Christianity

    Christianity, rooted in the teachings of Jesus Christ, is a monotheistic Abrahamic religion. The Bible serves as its sacred scripture, detailing Christ's life, teachings, and resurrection. With diverse denominations, Christianity is one of the world's major religions, emphasizing love, forgiveness, and salvation.

    Irreligion

    Irreligion encompasses a range of beliefs rejecting or lacking adherence to religious doctrines. Atheism denies the existence of deities, agnosticism asserts uncertainty about their existence, and secular humanism promotes ethical values without religious basis. Irreligion is diverse, reflecting a spectrum of non-religious perspectives and worldviews.

    Islam

    Islam is a monotheistic Abrahamic faith founded in the 7th century by Prophet Muhammad, as revealed in the Quran. The Five Pillars of Islam guide believers in worship of Allah, charity, fasting, pilgrimage, and faith. Islam is practiced by over a billion people worldwide, with diverse traditions and interpretations.

    Buddhism

    Buddhism, founded by Siddhartha Gautama in the 6th century BCE, emphasizes the Four Noble Truths, acknowledging suffering and advocating its cessation through the Eightfold Path. The teachings are preserved in the Tripitaka and various schools of thought, such as Theravada and Mahayana, have developed.

    Hinduism

    Hinduism, one of the world's oldest religions, lacks a single founder and encompasses diverse beliefs and practices. Sacred texts like the Vedas and Upanishads guide followers, emphasizing concepts such as dharma (moral duty) and karma (action and consequence).

    Folk religion

    Folk religion, rooted in indigenous beliefs, blends animism and ancestor veneration. Passed down orally, it reflects local traditions, shaping diverse spiritual systems. Grounded in shared experiences, it provides identity and connection to natural and ancestral realms in various cultural contexts.
